Capacity constraints:Each directed route (e.g., from City 0 to City 1) has a maximum allowable flow, called its capacity. The flow on that arc must be between 0 and this capacity. These constraints prevent overallocation on any link, reflecting real-world limits like road or pipeline capacity.
Flow conservation:For each city, the total inflow minus total outflow must equal the city's net supply/demand.
