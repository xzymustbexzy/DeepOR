Terminology Interpreter: ```inventory flow balance:This is a core constraint in dynamic lot-sizing and production planning problems. It ensures material flow consistency across periods. For period 1, it's defined as: Ordered amount (Q1) - Ending Inventory (I1) = Demand (D1). For periods t >= 2, the general form is: Q_t + I_{t-1} - I_t - B_{t-1} + B_t = D_t, where I is ending inventory (non-negative) and B is backlog (non-negative). This equation balances supply (new orders + prior inventory) with demand and stock status (ending inventory or new backlog), ensuring all demand is eventually met, either from current stock or via backlogging.
binary decision variable (indicating whether an order is placed):This variable activates the fixed ordering cost and the capacity constraint for a given period. Typically denoted as y_t ∈ {0, 1}, it enforces a logical link: if y_t = 0, then the order quantity Q_t must be 0; if y_t = 1, then Q_t can be positive up to a maximum (here, 8,231 units). This creates a mixed-integer programming (MIP) structure, making the problem NP-hard. The fixed cost for period t is incurred only if y_t = 1.
backlogging costs:This is the penalty cost per unit per period for unmet demand that is postponed to a future period. Unlike a lost sale, backlogged demand must be fulfilled later. In the flow balance, backlog B_t represents the amount of period t's demand that remains unsatisfied at the end of period t. The cost incentivizes meeting demand promptly. The interplay between backlogging cost, holding cost, and variable ordering cost determines the optimal trade-off between delaying orders, carrying inventory, and delaying customer fulfillment.
